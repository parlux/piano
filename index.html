Hanon variations, for reference

Pick two per session, not all seven. Pair them to the day's flaw focus.

01
Plain, slow, full weight
Baseline control — deep key contact, even tone bed-to-bed.
02
Dotted rhythm (long–short)
Exposes and corrects unevenness. Core tool for Baroque-evenness days.
03
Reverse dotted (short–long)
Flips which note carries the weight — catches unevenness the first version hides.
04
Voiced RH (right loud, left quiet)
Isolates RH control without LH masking it.
05
Voiced LH (left loud, right quiet)
Main pinky-control tool. Watch the shoulder while doing this one.
06
Left hand only
Removes RH entirely — easier to feel, and to film, LH weight distribution and shoulder tension.
07
Light staccato
Quick, minimal-weight release. Cross-check against the shoulder drill — controlled here but heavy in legato points to a release/drop issue rather than pure tension.
